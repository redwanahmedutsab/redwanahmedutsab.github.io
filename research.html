CCTV Footage: Live Threat & Suspicious Movement Detection

Authors: Redwan Ahmed Utsab, Bushra Jannat, Azizur Rahman Anik

Not yet published

Abstract: This research introduces a deep learning-based system for real-time detection of suspicious activities in CCTV footage. Leveraging Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and motion tracking algorithms, the system can identify potential security threats such as loitering, trespassing, and unauthorized access. The approach incorporates data augmentation and temporal consistency checks to ensure high accuracy in diverse environmental conditions. Initial tests demonstrate a detection accuracy exceeding 94%, with low false-positive rates, making it suitable for security-critical environments.

Retrieving Information from Long Text by Optimizing Transformer Architecture

Authors: Redwan Ahmed Utsab, Nafi Sadman Khan, Rafi Abrar Kabir, Md. Koushik Ahmmed, Junaid Rahman Mokit, Dr. Md. Nurul Huda, Mr. Nahid Hossain

Not yet published

Abstract: Large Language Models (LLMs) based on Transformer architecture have revolutionized NLP tasks but face challenges with long input sequences due to high memory consumption and inconsistent attention distribution. This research investigates architectural optimizations to enhance scalability and accuracy for long-context understanding. Techniques such as linear attention mechanisms, recurrent modules, and state-space models (SSMs) are explored. Results demonstrate improved contextual retention and inference efficiency, making the model suitable for complex document analysis, summarization, and content retrieval tasks.

TEXT-to-Image Generation for Circuit Design

Authors: Redwan Ahmed Utsab, Nafi Sadman Khan, Mr. Nahid Hossain

Not yet published

Abstract: This study presents an AI-driven framework capable of generating circuit diagrams from textual descriptions. The system integrates Natural Language Processing (NLP) for text interpretation and Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) for image generation. By translating functional specifications into visual circuit representations, the framework aims to accelerate prototyping and reduce manual errors in electrical design. Preliminary results show accurate mapping from textual input to schematic diagrams, enabling rapid conceptualization and testing.
